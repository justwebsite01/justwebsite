Registration Is NOT Optional

Under Section 55 of the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, any agreement for leave and licence or letting of any premises, entered into between the after the commencement of this Act, shall be registered under the Registration Act, 1908 (XVI of 1908). The responsibility of getting such agreement registered shall be on the landlord and in the absence of the written registered agreement, Any landlord who contravenes the provisions of this section shall, on conviction, be punished with imprisonment which may extend to three months or with fine not exceeding rupees five thousand or with both. Therfore every Leave and License Agreement must be compulsorily registered with the Sub-Registrar's Office. An unregistered agreement holds limited evidentiary value in courts and cannot protect a licensor's right to recover possession. Protect yourself — register today.

A lease, on the other hand, transfers a legal interest and can attract Rent Control Act protection. L&L agreements are therefore preferred by property owners in Maharashtra as they ensure easy recovery of possession upon expiry.

In Maharashtra, stamp duty for L&L agreements is calculated as follows: (Monthly License fee + Average Monthly Security Deposit) × Number of Months of the Agreement, and then a percentage is applied as per the Maharashtra Stamp Act. For a typical 11-month agreement, stamp duty is usually a modest amount.
